# Morning Briefing Source Priorities

Use these source priorities when the user requests the daily briefing.

## Ranking rules

Score relevance in this order:

1. Direct relevance to Daneek's interests
   - AI
   - cybersecurity
   - tech stocks / major tech companies
   - Harrisonburg / Shenandoah Valley local news
   - one top national story
2. Freshness
   - prefer same-day or last-24h items
3. Signal over chatter
   - prefer reported developments, advisories, filings, outages, product launches, earnings-impacting moves, elections, public-safety events
4. Source quality
   - prefer direct reporting or official agency/company sources over commentary
5. Actionability
   - prefer stories that imply something to watch, verify, or do

## Output selection guidance

- Top Stories: choose the 3-5 most important items overall, not just the most dramatic.
- Interesting Threads: choose genuinely insightful threads or repeated-source patterns; do not fill this section with weak filler.
- Tech News: prefer items that matter for AI, semis, cloud, cyber, or major tech equities.
- Local News: prefer civic, safety, infrastructure, education, election, weather, or high-interest community items.

## Anti-noise rules

Avoid:
- repeating the same story in multiple sections unless there is a strong reason
- padding sections to hit max bullets
- vague bullets with no consequence
- over-reliance on a single wire source unless the story is truly dominant
